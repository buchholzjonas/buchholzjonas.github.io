1

We have told, to the extent that we know, about the nature of sacred Civāttāṉam, which is surrounded by tanks with flowers that, when the bees stir them up with their legs, burst open their buds and blossom, spitting out nectar from all the mouths that are their long petals. [Now] we will tell about the qualities of the site that is Maṇikaṇṭīccaram in the area of Kāñci, where the scholars, in various assemblies, study Tamil in all the places of learning, making even ambrosia taste bitter.
