"Vikaṭacakra! Wise one who cannot be grasped by the Vedas! You whose musth surpasses [in darkness] the excellence of the clouds! True hero who bestowed his grace on him whose eyes are like lotuses (= Viṣṇu)!1 Offspring of Umā, who has breasts that are like round pots or cakravāka birds!" When the four [classes of people] who have the right to sacred service, beginning with the Mayēccurar (Māheśvaras), said: "Give!", Civañāṉa Mātavaṉ, who lives in (Tiru-)Āvaṭutuṟai that is surrounded by fragrant groves and who has flawlessly studied the knowledge that dispells the enemy that is rebirth and the other forms of learning, told the Kāñcīmāhātmya (or: the greatness of Kāñci), which destroys the enemy that is ignorance, in the common Southern Language (i.e., Tamil).
