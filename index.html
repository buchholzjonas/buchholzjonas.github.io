1
O sages with reddish matted locks! We have spoken of the excellence of the great and truthful site that is Cattiyaviratam, surrounded by tanks with nectar-filled flowers. [Now], with joy, we shall tell of the excellence of enchanting Puṇṇiyakōṭi in the city of Kacci (Kāñci). Listen with concentration!

To the south of the great site that is Cattiyaviratam, with its groves where lightning flashes, lies Puṇṇiyakōṭi, a site of our God, beside Puṇṇiyatīrttam, which cannot be approached by cruel people. There, the realization of Śiva (civappēṟu) comes easily even to people like me.

In an excellent kalpa of the one who abides on the lotus flower (Brahmā), named Meghavāhana, Nārāyaṇa (Viṣṇu), praised by the seven worlds, assumed the form of a cloud and, with the wish to create the seven worlds, beginning with the lord on the lotus (Brahmā), bore the king of the celestials (Śiva) for a long time.
